Intro

In 2017, we rolled out the Changemaker idea to more schools and communities in Bangalore. We named the Challenge the Every Inch Upstream Changemaker Challenge and in October 2017 invited schools and communities across Bangalore to participate.
Over 45 teams submitted their changemaking ideas in Round 1. The key was that each team found solutions to problems that they could implement – in their circle of influence, and were mentored by a team selected adult mentor. 21 teams were shortlisted, attended a design thinking workshop at UB City and presented their project pitches in Round 2 on Nov 4th 2017.
11 teams progressed to Round 3 and were given further funding and guidance More information on 2017 Challenge can be found here

On Jan 19 2018, the 11 finalist teams showcased the changemaking they had accomplished. We were impressed by their passion and the progress they had made in creating change. The 11 finalist teams also evaluated each other’s performance along with the judges. Each team excelled in different areas of impact such as:
  • Team Safeleaf (Inventure) which helped make school events as close to zero waste as possible
  • Team Green the Red (Inventure) which encourages sustainable menstruation
  • Team Alpha Byte (Candor) and empoWer (NPS) which are undertaking skilling programs for school helpers (didis, security guards etc)
  • Team Parikrma from Parikrma which taught English, math, health and hygiene skills to children of construction labourers
  • Team Aquafighters (Inventure) which helps save water by installing water aerators in homes
  • Team Reach Out (Inventure) which helps teens with mental health issues
  • Team Circuit Breakers (TVS Academy, Hosur) which reduced dengue mosquitoes in their neighbourhood
  • Team Red Dot (Inventure)which educates underprivileged girls about menstruation and encourages sustainable menstruation
  • Team Binfinity (Inventure) which has created an app to teach users proper waste segregation and can be used by apartment communities
  • Team Solution Squad (DPS East) which has made their school a properly waste segregating community and is reaching out to more institutions in their neighbourhood
